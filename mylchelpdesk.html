OneLogin User Guide

This guide walks you through logging in for the first time and setting up authentication.

First-Time Login

  1. Go to leecollege.onelogin.com.
  2. Enter your Lee College email address and click Continue.
  3. Enter your network password and click Continue.
  4. You'll be prompted to set up 2-Factor Authentication. Click Begin Setup.

Authentication Options

  • OneLogin Protect App: Recommended. Scan the QR code using the app.
  • SMS Text: Receive a code via text.
  • Authenticator App: Use another preferred app like Google Authenticator.
  • Email option coming soon.

After Setup

  • Log in to apps like Blackboard, Email, or MyLC Campus through the OneLogin dashboard.
  • You may not see all apps at first — they’ll appear as integrations are completed.
  • To reset your password later, click your name at the top right and select "Profile."

Need more help? Visit www.lee.edu/logins.

Clearing Browser Cache Guide

This guide will help you clear your browser cache to resolve common loading or access issues when using Blackboard or other campus tools.

Recommended Browsers: For best results, use Chrome or Firefox on a computer.

Chrome

  1. Click the three-dot menu at the top-right of your browser window.
  2. Select Delete browsing data.
  3. Choose a time range such as All time.
  4. Check only Cached images and files.
  5. Click Delete data.
  6. Refresh your browser and return to page to continue.

Firefox

  1. Click the menu button at the top-right and select Settings.
  2. Go to Privacy & Security in the left menu.
  3. Click Clear Data under the Cookies and Site Data section.
  4. Check only Cached Web Content.
  5. Click Clear and then refresh your browser.

Safari (Mac)

  1. Click Safari in the top menu bar and select Preferences.
  2. Go to the Privacy tab.
  3. Click Manage Website Data.
  4. Select the websites or choose Remove All.
  5. Click Done, then restart Safari and try Blackboard again.

Respondus LockDown Browser Guide

Lee College instructors may require to use the Respondus LockDown Browser (RLB) for quizzes and tests in Blackboard. This secure browser prevents copying, printing, or accessing other materials during an exam.

Download Respondus

To download the LockDown Browser, use the link below for Lee College students:

Click here to download Respondus LockDown Browser

How to Install

  1. Click the link above and select Install Now.
  2. Once downloaded, double-click the file and follow the installation instructions.
  3. Look for the “LockDown Browser” shortcut on your desktop.

Taking a Test with RLB

  1. Launch the LockDown Browser shortcut.
  2. Log into Blackboard and navigate to your course.
  3. Go to the content area where the test is located.
  4. Click the assessment link and read all instructions carefully.
  5. Click Begin to start the test. Keep track of the timer and save your answers as you go.
  6. Click Save and Submit when you’re done.

Taking a Monitored Test

If your instructor enables monitoring:

  • Make sure you have a webcam and a stable (preferably wired) internet connection.
  • You’ll be asked to verify your ID and complete a checklist before the test begins.
  • Look for “(**Webcam**)” in the test title to identify monitored exams.
  • After completing the checklist, your test will begin just like a regular RLB exam.

Tip: Avoid using public Wi-Fi during monitored exams. A wired connection is recommended.

Microsoft 365 & OneDrive Guide

As a Lee College student or faculty member, you have full access to Microsoft 365 apps — including Outlook, Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and OneDrive — from any device.

How to Access Microsoft 365 Apps

  1. Go to www.lee.edu.
  2. Click the Logins option in the top-right menu.
  3. Select Office 365 from the list of login services.
  4. Click the Office 365 Online Apps button.
  5. Enter your Lee College email address and click Next.
  6. Enter your Lee College network password and click Sign In.
  7. Choose whether to stay signed in (recommended only on personal computers).
  8. You will be directed to the Microsoft 365 dashboard, where you can access apps like:
    • Outlook – for your student email
    • Word, Excel, PowerPoint – for school assignments
    • OneDrive – for secure file storage
    • Teams, OneNote, and more

Once you're signed in, you can launch any app directly from the web, or choose to install the desktop version on your device.

What Is OneDrive?

OneDrive is your personal cloud storage space — like a digital backpack. You can save and organize all your school files, photos, and documents, and access them from any device, anywhere. You can also collaborate in real time with classmates and teachers.

How to Access OneDrive

  1. Go to www.lee.edu.
  2. Click Logins in the top-right menu.
  3. Choose Office 365 from the login options.
  4. Select Office 365 Online Apps.
  5. Enter your Lee College email, then click Next.
  6. Enter your network password and click Sign In.
  7. Choose whether to stay signed in (recommended only on personal computers).
  8. Click the OneDrive icon to access your files. If it's not visible, use the search bar to find it.

Creating Folders

After signing in:

  1. Click + Add New in OneDrive.
  2. Select Folder, name it, and click Create.

Uploading Files

Method 1: Drag and Drop

  1. Open OneDrive in a window and your files in another.
  2. Select and drag files into OneDrive. The upload will start automatically.

Method 2: Upload Button

  1. Click Upload in OneDrive.
  2. Select Files or Folder, choose what to upload, then click Open.

Sharing Files

  1. Hover over the file/folder you want to share and click the Share icon.
  2. In the pop-up window, select your sharing settings:
    • People in Lee College with the link (recommended).
    • Specific people to limit access outside Lee College.
  3. Choose whether users can Edit or only View.
  4. You can also prevent downloads by unchecking editing and choosing block download.
  5. Click Apply, then enter email addresses or use Copy Link to share.

Web Conferencing Guide

Lee College supports two main tools for attending live classes or virtual meetings: WebEx and Microsoft Teams. These tools are accessible through your Microsoft 365 account and allow you to attend lectures, participate in group assignments, or present assignments online.

Using WebEx

WebEx is used for live online classes or instructor-led presentations. Your instructor will provide a WebEx link inside your Blackboard course.

Accessing WebEx through OneLogin

  1. Go to the Lee College OneLogin portal.
  2. Log in with your Lee College email and password.
  3. On the dashboard, select the WebEx app icon.
  4. You will be taken directly to the WebEx platform where you can join or host meetings in your browser — no download needed.

If your instructor has scheduled a session, you may also see the WebEx link inside your Blackboard course under Course Content.

How to Join a WebEx Session

  1. Navigate to your Blackboard course and select the Course Content area.
  2. Click the WebEx link provided by your instructor.
  3. Click Join for the session you are attending.
  4. If prompted, choose to Open in WebEx App (recommended) or Join from browser.

Downloading WebEx

Although you can use WebEx in your browser through OneLogin, installing the app gives you better performance and access to all features.

  1. Visit the Cisco WebEx Download Page.
  2. Select the correct download for your computer or mobile device.
  3. Install and open the WebEx application.

WebEx Layout Overview

  • Top: Main control panel
  • Top right: Switch view controls
  • Right panel: Participants and chat tools
  • Bottom: Mute/unmute, start video, share screen or content

Chatting During a WebEx Session

  1. Select the Chat button or expand the chat panel.
  2. Choose Everyone to message all participants.
  3. Type your message and press Enter to send.

Sharing Your Screen or Files

  1. Click the Share button at the bottom of the session window.
  2. Select the application or screen you want to share.
  3. For videos, choose “Optimize for motion and video” before sharing.

Whiteboard and File Sharing

  1. Click Share, then scroll to find Whiteboard or File.
  2. Use Whiteboard to draw or annotate content during your session.

Stop Sharing

When finished, click the red Stop Sharing button at the top of your shared screen.

Additional Features for Instructors

Breakout Groups

Your instructor may assign you to breakout rooms for group activities. You'll have presenter controls and can share content with your group. Once the session ends, you’ll return to the main room automatically.

Polls

Instructors may launch polls during class. When active, polls appear on your screen, and results may be shared afterward. Look for the poll icon near the raise-hand button.

Accessing Microsoft Teams

  1. Go to www.office.com.
  2. Sign in with your Lee College email and password.
  3. Select the Teams icon from the Microsoft 365 app launcher.
  4. You can also download the Microsoft Teams desktop or mobile app for additional features.

Use Teams to message classmates, attend online meetings, or collaborate on shared documents.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is OneLogin?

OneLogin is Lee College’s centralized login portal for accessing most campus resources, including Blackboard, email, Microsoft applications, and more (excluding MyLC).

I’m enrolled in classes but still don’t have access to OneLogin. When will it be available?

OneLogin accounts are typically created within 36–48 hours after enrollment. If you still do not have access after 48 hours or have an urgent need, please contact the help desk by phone for immediate assistance.

I no longer receive Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) codes on my device. How can I resolve this?

Please contact the Help Desk by phone. We’ll help you reset your MFA and set up a new authentication method.

My login credentials aren’t working and I can’t reset my password. What should I do?

Call the help desk or visit us in person. We’ll verify your information and help you regain access.

I need help installing Microsoft Office on my personal computer. How can I get assistance?

We recommend visiting us in person for hands-on support. While phone assistance is available, in-person help ensures we can assist you more effectively with installation.

My myLC Campus password isn’t working. What should I try?

Try the default password given to you when you first enrolled. If that doesn’t work, your account may be locked. Contact the help desk to unlock or reset your account.

I can't access my eBooks in Blackboard. What should I do?

Your instructor should have posted instructions on accessing your eBook in the course. Make sure you have created an account on the publisher website using your Lee College credentials. If you are still experiencing issues, contact the help desk and we’ll connect you with our on-campus Blackboard Specialists for further support.

I’m having trouble accessing course-related websites on my Apple device. What can I do to resolve this issue?

Safari can have compatibility issues with certain websites. We recommend installing Google Chrome or Mozilla Firefox for improved access to all course-related websites.
